# Historical Cocktail Research - Skill Enhancement
## Sources from 1910-1917 Era

### Key Historical Sources Reviewed

1. **Tom Bullock - "The Ideal Bartender" (1917)**
   - Author worked at Pendennis Club (Louisville) and St. Louis Country Club
   - Perfect match for our skill's setting
   - Published just 2 years after our 1910-1915 timeframe

2. **Harry Johnson - "Bartender's Manual" (1882, 1888, 1900)**
   - Definitive guide for professional bartending
   - 1900 edition is perfect for our timeframe
   - Contains first written Martini recipe (1888)
   - Emphasis on professionalism, cleanliness, politeness

#### **Bartending Philosophy & Professional Standards:**

From Harry Johnson's manuals (1882-1900):

**Presentation & Technique:**
- "Stand straight, carry head erect, place himself in fine position"
- "Be neat, clean, and tidy in dress"
- "Be pleasant and cheerful with everyone"
- "Mix drinks above the counter so customers can see"
- "Prepare drinks in a neat, quick and scientific way to draw attention"
- "Everything used with drinks must be perfectly clean, glasses bright and polished"
- Set a glass of ice water before customer, then politely inquire what they desire

**Professional Conduct:**
- "Treat all customers with the utmost politeness and respect"
- Bartending is a respectable profession requiring skill and knowledge
- Pride in one's craft and establishment
- Discretion and proper behavior toward patrons
